Every account and every organization on Nubo has a public profile at `withnubo.com/@handle`. It is a simple builder page: your name, a short bio, your avatar, the date you joined, the Frames you have chosen to make public, your recent deployments, and an activity heatmap that fills in as you ship.

It is one identity for people and organizations alike. A personal account and a standard [organization](/organizations) each get their own `@handle` and their own page, built the same way.

## Turning it on

There are two switches, and both are yours to control.

**Publish your profile.** Go to **Settings > Profile** and turn on the public-profile toggle. Until you flip it on, nothing is shown publicly. Once it is on, anyone with the link can visit your page at `withnubo.com/@handle`.

**Make a Frame public.** Every [Frame](/frames) is Private by default, and only Public Frames appear on a profile. Open a Frame's **Settings > Visibility** and switch it to Public. Private Frames stay visible only to people with access to the Project.

<Note>
  Changing a Frame's visibility requires an organization admin. If you are not an admin of the owning organization, ask one to make the Frame public or private for you.
</Note>

## What stays private

A profile only ever shows what you deliberately opt in. Project ids, [environment variables](/environment-variables), email addresses, and internal hostnames are never part of the page, so nothing sensitive is exposed just because a profile is live.

The two defaults are worth remembering together. An organization's profile page is public by default, but its Frames are Private by default. That means a fresh profile shows your name and bio and nothing else until you make a Frame public on purpose.
